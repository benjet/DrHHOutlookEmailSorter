**Review my Outlook inbox only.**

**Your task is to identify emails or threads in the Inbox that currently have no category assigned and classify each one using the single best category only.**

**Scope and rules:**

**1\. Only review messages in the Inbox.**

**2\. Only review emails or threads that currently have no category assigned.**

**3\. Treat the conversation thread, not just the latest message, as the unit of classification.**

**4\. Do not classify based solely on subject line, preview text, or the latest message snippet. Review enough of the thread to determine the true current status.**

**5\. For each thread, determine whether:**

   **\- it needs a response or action from me**

   **\- it is informational only and does not require a response**

   **\- I have already replied and am now waiting on someone else**

   **\- I have already replied and the matter is complete**

   **\- the status is unclear or confidence is low**

**6\. Assign the single best category only. Do not apply more than one category to the same email/thread.**

**7\. If the email/thread needs a response or action from me, add category “1: Needs Response.”**

**8\. If the email/thread is informational only and does not require a response or action from me, add category “2: FYI.”**

**9\. If I have already replied and am now waiting on the other person, add category “3: Waiting for Reply.”**

**10\. If I have already replied and no further action is needed from me, or the matter is complete/closed, add category “4: Done.”**

**11\. Leave the email uncategorized if:**

   **\- confidence is low**

   **\- the thread is ambiguous**

   **\- it is unclear whether a response is needed**

   **\- it is unclear whether I already replied**

   **\- the thread contains mixed signals or insufficient context**

**12\. Do not assign “2: FYI” if there is any explicit or implied ask directed to me, even if the sender did not phrase it as a question.**

**13\. Never assign “2: FYI” if the email or thread includes an attachment, deadline, request for review, or invitation directed to me unless the thread clearly shows that no action is needed from me.**

**14\. Do not change read/unread status. If opening or touching an email causes its read/unread state to change, restore it to its original state before moving on.**

**15\. Preserve all existing properties other than the category update, including unread status, flags, reminders, and importance.**

**16\. Do not archive, delete, move, send, snooze, flag, or otherwise modify the email except for the category changes described above.**

**17\. Do not draft or rewrite any emails.**

**18\. Work thread by thread, using the most recent context in the conversation.**

**19\. Be conservative. If unsure, leave the email uncategorized.**

**20\. Prefer false negatives over false positives. It is better to leave an email uncategorized than to assign the wrong category.**

**Decision guidance:**

**\- Assign “1: Needs Response” when there is an explicit or implied ask directed to me and I still owe a reply or action.**

**\- Assign “2: FYI” when the thread is informational only, does not ask for a reply, and does not require action from me.**

**\- Assign “3: Waiting for Reply” when I have already replied and the next move belongs to someone else.**

**\- Assign “4: Done” when I have already replied and the thread appears resolved, closed, or no further action is needed from me.**

**\- Do not assign “4: Done” unless the thread clearly appears complete.**

**\- If my reply status cannot be confirmed with confidence, leave uncategorized.**

**Only use these categories:**

**\- “1: Needs Response”**

**\- “2: FYI”**

**\- “3: Waiting for Reply”**

**\- “4: Done”**
